Hannah Sherwood

Welcome to my Homepage!

I am a PhD student with the CDT Bioenergy at the University of Leeds and have an MSc in Climate Change and Environmnetal Policy and an undergraduate background in Geography.

My research project focuses on sustainable use of bioenergy in tea production landscapes in Kenya and is funded by EPSRC. For this project I am working in partnership with UK tea suppliers Taylor's of Harrogate and am affiliated with the United Bank of Carbon (UBoC).

This personal site hosts a portfolio of my work and links to the associated Github repositories for the module GEOG 5990M with the School of Geography where I have been learning the basics of programming for geographic data analysis.
